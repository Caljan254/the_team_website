THE TEAM CONSTITUTION

Diverse but United

Official Group Constitution and By-Laws

Effective Date: January 1, 2026

All members are bound by this constitution upon joining the group

Table of Contents

★ PREAMBLE

We, the members of THE TEAM, recognizing the need for collective action in fighting poverty and improving our economic status, do hereby establish this Constitution to guide our operations, define our objectives, and regulate our conduct as a unified group.

1 ARTICLE 1: NAME

The group shall be known as "THE TEAM".

Motto: Diverse but United

2 ARTICLE 2: OBJECTIVES

The main objectives of the group shall be:

  • To promote a culture of saving among members through regular contributions
  • To provide financial assistance to members through affordable loans
  • To undertake income-generating projects and investments for the benefit of members
  • To support members during emergencies and difficult times
  • To promote unity, cooperation, and mutual understanding among members
  • To educate members on financial management and investment opportunities
  • To create a social support network for members' welfare

3 ARTICLE 3: MEMBERSHIP

  • Membership is open to all willing individuals who accept the terms of this constitution
  • Registration fee: KSh 200 (non-refundable)
  • Monthly contribution: KSh 600 payable by the 10th of every month
  • All members must attend monthly meetings (minimum 75% attendance required)
  • Members must maintain regular contributions to remain in good standing
  • Members can withdraw from the group after 5 years of continuous membership
  • New members must be introduced by an existing member
  • Membership can be terminated for:
    • Non-payment of contributions for 3 consecutive months
    • Gross misconduct
    • Violation of group rules and regulations

4 ARTICLE 4: OFFICIALS

  • Chairperson: Overall leadership, presides over meetings, represents the group
  • Treasurer: Manages finances, keeps financial records, issues receipts
  • Secretary: Record keeping, minutes, correspondence, membership records
  • Organizing Secretary: Organizes meetings and group activities
  • Term of Office: 2 years, renewable once
  • Elections shall be held during the Annual General Meeting
  • Officials can be removed by 2/3 majority vote for misconduct or negligence

5 ARTICLE 5: MEETINGS

  • Monthly Meetings: Held on the 11th of every month at 2:00 PM
  • Emergency Meetings: Can be called by the Chairperson or 1/3 of members
  • Annual General Meeting (AGM): Held in December each year
  • Quorum: 2/3 of members must be present for decisions to be binding
  • Minutes: Must be kept for all meetings and read in subsequent meetings
  • Decisions are made by simple majority vote unless otherwise specified
  • Members must give advance notice if unable to attend

6 ARTICLE 6: FINANCES

  • Monthly contributions: KSh 600 per member
  • Due date: 10th of every month
  • Late payment penalty: KSh 10 per day after the deadline
  • Registration fee: KSh 200 (paid once on joining)
  • Loan application fee: KSh 100
  • All payments to be made via M-Pesa to official group account
  • Treasurer must keep proper records and issue receipts
  • Financial statements to be presented in every monthly meeting
  • Annual audit to be conducted in December
  • Group funds to be kept in a bank account requiring two signatories

7 ARTICLE 7: LOANS

  • Maximum loan amount: KSh 50,000
  • Minimum loan amount: KSh 1,000
  • Repayment period: 3 months maximum
  • Interest rate: 10% per month on reducing balance
  • Eligibility: Must have contributed for 3 consecutive months
  • Guarantor required for loans above KSh 20,000
  • Maximum of two active loans per member at any time
  • Loan applications must be submitted in writing
  • Loan committee to review and approve applications
  • Default penalties: Additional 5% interest for late repayment

8 ARTICLE 8: WITHDRAWAL

  • Members can withdraw after 5 years of continuous membership
  • Withdrawal window: December only
  • Notice period: 3 months written notice required
  • Withdrawing member receives all contributions plus accrued interest
  • Interest on savings: 5% per annum
  • Outstanding loans must be cleared before withdrawal
  • Withdrawal form must be completed and approved by committee
  • Final payment made within 30 days of approval

9 ARTICLE 9: COMMITTEES

  • Executive Committee: Overall management of group affairs
  • Loan Committee: Reviews and approves loan applications
  • Disciplinary Committee: Handles disputes and disciplinary matters
  • Projects Committee: Identifies and manages income-generating projects
  • Committee members serve for 1 year
  • Each committee to have at least 3 members
  • Committee decisions to be ratified in general meetings

10 ARTICLE 10: DISCIPLINE

  • Members must conduct themselves with integrity and respect
  • Disciplinary actions include:
    • Verbal warning
    • Written warning
    • Temporary suspension
    • Expulsion
  • Disciplinary committee to handle all disciplinary matters
  • Right to appeal decisions within 14 days
  • Expulsion requires 3/4 majority vote

11 ARTICLE 11: AMENDMENTS

This constitution can be amended by 2/3 majority of members present in a special meeting called for that purpose. Proposed amendments must be submitted in writing at least 14 days before the meeting. All amendments become effective immediately upon approval unless otherwise specified.

12 ARTICLE 12: DISSOLUTION

In the event of dissolution, all assets shall be liquidated and distributed to members in proportion to their contributions. Dissolution requires approval by 90% of all members. A dissolution committee shall be formed to oversee the process.

IMPORTANT NOTES

  • This constitution supersedes all previous constitutions and agreements
  • All members are bound by this constitution upon joining
  • Copies of this constitution are available to all members
  • Interpretation of this constitution rests with the Executive Committee
  • Any matter not covered herein shall be decided by the Executive Committee
